DISTCULT: AlgoMech Panel on Distributed Culture

Join in person or online:

  • Beunos Aires (AR): Centro Cultural de la Cienca - free, no signup required
  • Sheffield (UK): DINA - free, signup required
  • Watch online

Time: 18:00-20:00 UTC, Saturday 29th February 2020 (click for your local time)

Speakers

  • Marta Dillon, co-founder of Ni Una Menos (not one woman less)
    speaking on the influential Ni Una Menos fourth-wave feminist movement and protest group, including its origins in Argentina, and use of the internet in its spread across the world.
  • Julian Oliver, independent artist and critical engineer
    talking about his experience building the server infrastructure for Extinction Rebellion.
    Previously: Server Infrastructure for Global Rebellion, 36th CCC, 2019
  • Richard Parncutt, University of Graz
    discussing how to change academic culture in response to climate emergency, from his experience co-ordinating a low-carbon, multi-hub international conference (ICMPC 2018), as well as his plans to produce a global choral festival.
    Previously: Low-carbon, multi-hub global conferencing: Balancing the real and the virtual, Wegener Center for Climate and Global Change, 2018

Chaired/organised by Alex McLean and Iris Saladino.

[EN] This panel will explore the past, present and future of distributed events, taking input from artists, scientists and activists. We will explore ways of making use of the internet to connect communities, to create international events that are inclusive, diverse and promote cultural exchange. This discussion will be against the backdrop of ecological breakdown, while looking to take on the challenge of making new spaces for cultural exchange. It will close with an international performance from members of the live coding community in Argentina, Brasil, Indonesia and more.

[ES] Este panel explorará el pasado, el presente y el futuro de los eventos distribuidos, teniendo en cuenta las aportaciones de artistas, científicos y activistas. Exploraremos formas de hacer uso de Internet para conectar a las comunidades, crear eventos internacionales que sean inclusivos, diversos y promuevan el intercambio cultural. Esta discusión tendrá como telón de fondo el colapso ecológico, mientras busca asumir el desafío de crear nuevos espacios para el intercambio cultural. Se cerrará con una actuación internacional de miembros de la comunidad de codificación en vivo en Argentina, Brasil, Indonesia y más.

Respondents

In the days following the event, an online forum will open to explore themes and outcomes from the panel, in terms of designing future online events. A focus will be on alternative approaches to online events, particularly those exploring the algorithmic and digital arts and related research topics. All are welcome to join this discussion, which will include invited responses from the following:

  • Nora O Murchú (Artistic Director, Transmediale festival#, Berlin)
  • Akihiro Kubota (Professor, Tama Art University, Tokyo)
  • Amber Griffiths (Director, FoAM Kernow, UK)
  • David Ogborn (Director, Networked Imagination Laboratory, Hamilton University, CA)
  • Jake Harries, Access Space Sheffield/Little piece of land, UK
  • Abhinay Khoparzi, Algorave India
  • Alejandro Albornoz Rojas, Lecturer and Researcher, Universidad Austral de Chile, Valdivia
